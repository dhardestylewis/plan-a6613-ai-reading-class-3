Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) and Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR)

Source: SBIR.gov (https://www.sbir.gov/about)

Program Definition:
America's Seed Fund. Provides equity-free funding through federal agencies to American small businesses to develop technology and chart a path toward commercialization.

Key Mechanisms:
1. Non-dilutive Funding:
   - Awards funding without taking equity (equity-free).
   - "Seed Fund" model.
   - Supports early-stage technology funding.

2. Ecosystem Coordination:
   - Coordinated by the creation of the Small Business Administration (SBA).
   - Funded through 11 participating federal agencies.
   - Involves "Support Organizations" (Accelerators, technical assistance centers).

3. Three-Phase Program (Implicit in "develop... and chart a path toward commercialization"):
   - Typically involves Phase I (Feasibility), Phase II (R&D), Phase III (Commercialization - though Phase III is non-SBIR funded usually, the path is the key).

4. Impact:
   - "High-impact, disruptive innovations."
   - Since 1982, has moved ideas from sparks to transformative technologies.
   - Focuses on R&D-focused entrepreneurs.

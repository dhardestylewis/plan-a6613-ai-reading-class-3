## American Action Forum (2025)
- Virginia SCC approved new GS-5 rate class for large electricity consumers (including data centers consuming >25 MW)
- Effective January 1, 2027
- Requirements: 14-year contracts; minimum demand commitments (85% of contracted distribution/transmission demand, 60% of generation demand)
- Intent: protect other ratepayers from costs of rapid infrastructure build-out for data centers

## Inside Climate News (2025)
- SCC approved Dominion Energy rate increase in November 2025
- Approximately $16/month increase for typical residential bill in 2026
- Base rate increases: $11.24/month in 2026, additional $2.36/month in 2027 (total $13.60/month)
- Represents 9% increase over two years

## 13NewsNow (2025)
- SB 253 introduced by State Senator Louise Lucas
- Would require data centers to pay for their own electrical substations and "capacity costs"
- Projected residential customer savings: >$5.50/month or >$65/year
- Would increase data center costs by ~16% while reducing residential costs

## Pew Research Center (2025) — via search summary
- U.S. data centers consumed 183 TWh in 2024, over 4% of national electricity
- Forecast: 133% growth to 426 TWh by 2030
- Carnegie Mellon University study: data centers could increase average U.S. electricity bill by 8% by 2030
- Increases exceeding 25% in heavily concentrated markets like Northern Virginia
- Natural gas supplied over 40% of U.S. data center electricity in 2024; renewables ~24%

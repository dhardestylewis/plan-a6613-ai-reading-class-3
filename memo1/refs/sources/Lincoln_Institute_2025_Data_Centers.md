# Lincoln Institute of Land Policy. (2025). Data Centers and Local Government.

## Source Type
Policy institute research/FAQ

## URL
https://www.lincolninst.edu/

## Key Data Points

### Loudoun County Fiscal Data
- Loudoun County anticipates ~$900 million in data center tax revenue in FY2025
- This sum approaches the county's entire operating budget
- Data centers generate 38% of all general fund revenues for Loudoun County
- County established a Revenue Stabilization Fund in 2023 to manage revenue fluctuations from data centers
- $40 million allocated to Revenue Stabilization Fund in proposed FY2025 budget

### Expert Analysis
- Mary Ann Dickinson (Lincoln Institute policy director for land and water): emphasizes need to help communities make "informed decisions and plan for the potential impacts of data centers on their structures and systems"
- Kim Rueben (former senior fiscal systems advisor, Lincoln Institute): compared the "extractive nature of data centers to coal mines," suggesting communities "may not fully acknowledge all the associated costs"

### Relevance to Our Thesis
The Lincoln Institute data strengthens the fiscal lock-in stage of our argument:
- $900M approaching entire operating budget = deeper dependency than previously cited $600M
- Revenue Stabilization Fund itself is evidence that the county KNOWS it's dependent and is trying to manage volatility — but the fund doesn't address the underlying lock-in
- The coal mine comparison (Rueben) aligns with Unruh's path dependency framework
- 38% of general fund = the county literally cannot function without DC tax revenue

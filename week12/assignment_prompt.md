AI Reading Exercise - Week 12
We will again test the power and limitations of modern AI tools for sourcing and synthesizing relevant information. As usual, the objective is to use an LLM to answer a core question related to this week's class session, while critically auditing the tool's output.

For this week's topic of "AI and City Life," we will use an LLM to investigate how AI is reshaping a specific dimension of everyday urban experience — and then audit the quality, balance, and accuracy of what the tool returns. Use an AI tool of your choice (e.g., Gemini, ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) to research the following question:

"How is AI currently changing the experience of [pick one: retail and shopping, entertainment and hospitality, or urban mobility and travel] in cities? What does the evidence say about who benefits from these changes — and what concerns have researchers, advocates, or urban planners raised about equity and access?"

**Requirements**
Please create a brief (max 1-page) document with bulleted responses to the following:

- **Tool & Process:** State which AI model you used and the specific prompt(s) you found most effective.
- **Key Findings:** A concise summary of how AI is being applied in your chosen domain, any reported shifts in consumer behavior or urban experience, and how the benefits and disruptions are distributed across different income levels, neighborhoods, or demographic groups.
- **Verification (The "Human-in-the-Loop" Check):** Provide three real-world citations (links to academic research, industry reports, urban policy analyses, or investigative journalism) that support these findings. Did the AI surface real, retrievable sources? Did it overstate how widespread or equitable these changes are, or ignore evidence of uneven impacts? Note any hallucinations or omissions here.
- **Critical Reflection:** In 2-3 sentences, rate the utility of the AI's response. Did it grapple seriously with the question of who wins and who loses as AI reshapes daily urban life, or did it present a uniformly rosy picture — and what does that suggest about using AI tools to research the social dimensions of AI adoption?

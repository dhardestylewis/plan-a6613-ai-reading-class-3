Source: Uptime Institute — 2023 Global Data Center Survey

Accessed: March 3, 2026
Type: Industry research (independent, non-vendor)
NOTE: Full report is behind membership paywall; executive summary available free

KEY DATA EXTRACTED:

PUE Data:
- Global average PUE of respondents' data centers: ~1.58 (2023)
- PUE has been "flatlining" in recent years
- Primary contributor to flatlining: richer geographical mix of surveyed DCs
  - More DCs in Asia, Middle East, Africa, Latin America
  - These regions: smaller capacity, warmer climates → higher energy consumption
- This is the source Google cites when claiming 1.10 PUE vs. 1.58 industry average

Survey Details:
- 13th year of the survey (longest-running study of its kind)
- Authors: Douglas Donnellan, Daniel Bizo, Jacqueline Davis, Andy Lawrence, Owen Rogers, Lenny Simon, Max Smolaks

AUDIT NOTES:
- The 1.58 industry average includes small, inefficient enterprise DCs in warmer climates
- Comparing Google's hyperscale PUE (1.10) against this average flatters hyperscalers
- A fairer comparison would be against other hyperscalers (AWS, Microsoft, Meta)
- The Uptime Institute is a respected independent source, but PUE is a limited metric:
  it measures infrastructure efficiency, not absolute energy consumption or emissions

# Annotation: IEA Data Centres and Data Transmission Networks
**Source**: International Energy Agency. (2023). Data centres and data transmission networks.
**URL**: https://www.iea.org/energy-system/buildings/data-centres-and-data-transmission-networks
**Stakeholder**: Intergovernmental (independent, authoritative)
**Conflict of interest**: None — IEA is an independent intergovernmental organization

## Key Extractions with Line References

### Global Emissions Context
- "Data centres and data transmission networks... accounted for around 330 Mt CO2 equivalent in 2020 (including embodied emissions), equivalent to 0.9% of energy-related GHG emissions" (Position 11, citing Masanet et al. 2020)
  - NOT used directly in writeup — provides global denominator context

### Hyperscaler Energy Growth
- "Combined electricity use by Amazon, Microsoft, Google, and Meta more than doubled between 2017 and 2021, rising to around 72 TWh in 2021." (Position 11)
  - Used in writeup: "combined electricity use by Amazon, Microsoft, Google, and Meta more than doubled between 2017 and 2021, reaching approximately 72 terawatt-hours" ✅

- Large data centres energy use "growing by 20-40% annually" (Position 11)
  - Used in writeup: "continues to grow 20 to 40 percent annually in the hyperscale segment" ✅

### Efficiency vs. Absolute Growth
- "Since 2010, data centre energy use (excluding crypto) has grown only moderately despite the strong growth in demand for data centre services, thanks in part to efficiency improvements" (Position 11)
  - Used implicitly in writeup: "Per-unit efficiency improvements are being overwhelmed by the absolute growth in AI workloads" ✅

### Net-Zero Scenario
- "to get on track with the NZE Scenario, emissions must halve by 2030" (Position 11)
  - Used in writeup: "IEA's requirement that data center emissions must halve by 2030 under a net-zero scenario" ✅

## What This Source Does NOT Address
- Google specifically (reports on industry aggregate)
- AI-specific energy consumption (reports on all data center workloads)
- Location-based vs. market-based accounting distinction
- Specific mitigation technologies (TPU, PUE comparisons)

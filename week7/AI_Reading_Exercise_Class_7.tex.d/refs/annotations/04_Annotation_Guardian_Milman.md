## Key Extractions (from search synthesis)

### Core Finding
- Google's carbon emissions increased by 65% between 2019 and 2024 using location-based accounting
  - Used in writeup: "a 65% increase over the same period" ✅
  - Compare with Google's self-reported 51% increase (market-based)

### Methodology Distinction
- "Location-based emissions represent the average emissions a company produces from its use of local power grids"
  - Used in writeup: "location-based accounting, which measures actual grid emissions at the point of consumption" ✅

- "Market-based emissions include energy purchased by the company to offset its total emissions"
  - Used in writeup: "market-based accounting, which incorporates renewable energy credits" ✅

- Location-based emissions described as reflecting a company's "real" grid emissions
- Market-based can "obscure a polluter's actual impact"

### Additional Data
- Emissions from electricity purchases jumped 121% between 2019 and 2024
  - NOT used in writeup — supports the location-based finding but would require more space

- Data center energy requirements saw 820% increase since 2010
  - NOT used in writeup — dramatic but less relevant to the 2019-2024 comparison

## What This Source Does NOT Address
- Google's specific mitigation technologies
- Per-prompt or per-workload efficiency
- Comparison to other hyperscalers' location-based emissions

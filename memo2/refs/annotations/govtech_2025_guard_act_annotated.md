# Annotation: GovTech (2025) — NYC GUARD Act

## Legislative Response [ACCOUNTABILITY]
- GUARD Act passed November 2025 by NYC City Council
- Full name: Guaranteeing Unbiased AI Regulation and Disclosure Act
- Establishes independent Office of Algorithmic Data Accountability → first of its kind for municipal AI oversight
- Mandatory pre-deployment assessments → if this had existed in 2023, MyCity chatbot would have required pre-deployment audit
- Public complaints mechanism + public directory of AI systems → transparency requirements

## Regulatory Gap [GOVERNANCE_FAILURE]
- The 2023 AI Action Plan that launched the chatbot relied on VOLUNTARY principles, not mandatory standards
- GUARD Act explicitly moves to MANDATORY standards for fairness, privacy, transparency, and independent evaluation
- The MyCity chatbot failure is effectively the catalyst for this legislation

## Existing Framework [ACCOUNTABILITY]
- Local Law 144 (2021/2023): bias audits for AI hiring tools → narrowly scoped, did not cover chatbots
- Local Law 35 (2022): annual reporting of automated decision systems → reporting only, no quality requirements
- Neither existing law would have prevented the MyCity chatbot deployment

## State-Level Liability [LOOKING_AHEAD]
- NY Senate Bill 7263 (proposed early 2026): creates liability for chatbot proprietors providing "substantive" advice equivalent to unauthorized practice of licensed professions
- Permits private rights of action for damages → would directly apply to MyCity-type deployments
- If enacted, government chatbots providing legal/regulatory advice would face the same liability as unlicensed legal practitioners

# Source: Fast Company (2024) — NYC MyCity Chatbot Controversy

**Type**: Business/Technology Journalism
**Date**: April 2024
**URL**: https://www.fastcompany.com/91103851/nyc-ai-chatbot-mycity-breaks-law

## Key Content

The Adams administration defended keeping the pilot program online, arguing that testing in a "real environment" was necessary to "iron out the kinks."

The city updated the portal to include more prominent warnings that the tool was in "beta," could provide inaccurate or incomplete information, and that its responses should not be treated as professional or legal advice.

Through 2024 and 2025, OTI officials continued to work on the system.

The chatbot was part of the broader MyCity portal, first launched in March 2023 to help families access child care, then extended to business compliance.

The "pilot program" framing was central to the administration's defense: citizens became unconsenting beta testers of a system delivering regulatory advice.

# Source: GovTech (2025) — NYC GUARD Act AI Oversight Legislation

**Type**: Government Technology Journalism
**Date**: November 2025
**URL**: https://www.govtech.com/artificial-intelligence/new-york-city-passes-ai-oversight-legislation

## Key Content

The NYC City Council passed the Guaranteeing Unbiased AI Regulation and Disclosure (GUARD) Act in November 2025.

The Act establishes an independent Office of Algorithmic Data Accountability, tasked with:
- Auditing, monitoring, and regulating AI tools used by city agencies
- Conducting pre-deployment assessments of AI systems
- Investigating public complaints regarding AI tools
- Publishing a public directory of all evaluated AI systems

The legislation moves beyond the previous voluntary principles found in the city's 2023 AI Action Plan by establishing mandatory citywide standards for fairness, privacy, transparency, and independent evaluation.

Existing regulatory framework includes:
- Local Law 144 (Bias Audit Law, 2021/enforced 2023): requires annual independent bias audits for AI hiring tools
- Local Law 35 (2022): requires city agencies to submit annual reports listing automated decision systems

## State-Level Context

New York State Senate Bill 7263 (proposed early 2026) would create liability for chatbot proprietors (including government entities) that provide "substantive" responses equivalent to unauthorized practice of licensed professions. The bill permits private rights of action for damages.

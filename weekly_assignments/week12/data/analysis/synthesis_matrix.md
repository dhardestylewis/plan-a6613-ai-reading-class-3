# Pre-Audit Source Matrix: Urban Mobility & AI

This matrix maps the four conflicting narratives regarding algorithmic deployment in mobility infrastructure, culminating in the Level 4 Causal Synthesis.

| Stakeholder / Viewpoint | Primary Argument | Mechanism of Disruption | View on "Equity" |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1 (Data Scientists)** | AI suffers from technical flaws. The algorithm *wants* to be fair but misinterprets historical discrimination. | Algorithmic "Feedback Loops" (FairST, 2022). | A mathematical variable solved by adding "fairness constraints." |
| **Level 2 (Regulators / FTC)** | The algorithms are *not* broken; they perfectly optimize for profit at the expense of vulnerable populations. | Opaque "Black Box" optimization creates digitized redlining (FTC, 2024). | Equity requires dismantling the "black box" and imposing audits. |
| **Level 3 (Spatial Epistemologists)** | Levels 1 and 2 overestimate the code. The algorithm is neither accidentally racist nor perfectly greedy; it is merely *fragile* in uncomputable space. | The **"Non-Stationary City"**: Algorithms collapse in the chaotic physical environment of a real city (ORE, 2022). | True harm is outsourcing infrastructure to brittle mathematical models that fundamentally cannot process reality. |
| **Level 4 (Causal Architect)** | **The Ultimate Delusion:** Levels 1, 2, and 3 wrongly treat the AI as an *observer* of the city. In reality, the AI *constructs* the city. | **The Terraforming Loop:** When an algorithm hallucinates "low demand," it physically starves the area of resources until the physical geography dies and matches the algorithm's prediction (Contesti, 2026). | Equity is impossible because the map violently destroys the territory. We have ceded reality-generating power to a mathematical loop. |

**Ultimate Synthesis Vector:** The debate over whether AI is "flawed," "greedy," or "fragile" is a distraction. The truest danger of Urban AI is its immense, unchecked causal power. It does not predict the marginalized city; it physically manufactures it.

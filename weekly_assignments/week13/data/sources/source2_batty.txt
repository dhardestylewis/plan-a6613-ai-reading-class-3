Title: Digital twins (2018)
Author: Michael Batty
Journal: Environment and Planning B: Urban Analytics and City Science, 45(5), 817-820.
URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/2399808318796416

"We must be careful not to confuse the model with the reality. Digital twins of cities, unlike digital twins of jet engines or manufacturing plants, contend with profound complexity and non-deterministic human behavior. When used for land-use planning, these models risk projecting historical biases into the future. A twin is not an objective replica; it is a highly selective abstraction determined by what data is deemed worth collecting and what is ignored. To assume that a singular digital model can perfectly simulate the urban condition is to misunderstand the fundamental, irreducible nature of city systems."

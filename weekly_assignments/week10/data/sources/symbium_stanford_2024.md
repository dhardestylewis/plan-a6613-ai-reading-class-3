**Publisher**: Symbium (symbium.com) / Stanford CodeX
**Founded by**: Leila Banijamali, Abhijeet Mohapatra, Professor Michael Genesereth
**Origin**: Stanford University's CodeX (Center for Legal Informatics) and Computer Science Department
**Type**: Gov-Tech Vendor / Academic Spinoff
**URL**: https://symbium.com

## Key Content (Verified via Symbium Website and Web Search)

### Product: Complaw®

1. Symbium's proprietary Complaw® technology uses "Computational Law" to translate complex local and state regulations, zoning codes, and building requirements into a digital, "TurboTax-like" workflow.

2. The system codifies legal requirements into a logical language (specifically utilizing logic programming) to verify compliance in real-time.

3. As applicants enter information about their project, the system instantly evaluates the proposal against applicable codes and provides immediate feedback.

4. The platform enables instant issuance of permits without the need for manual review or in-person visits in participating jurisdictions.

### Municipal Adoption

5. Symbium has partnered with numerous municipalities: San Mateo County, City of Bakersfield, City of Ventura, Mill Valley, San Juan Capistrano, among others.

6. Symbium helps municipalities meet California SB 379 requirements, which mandate that cities and counties provide an online, automated permitting platform for residential solar and energy storage systems.

7. Some systems launched in under a month. The platform integrates with existing permit tracking systems (e.g., Accela, Tyler Technologies).

8. While initially focused on solar/energy storage, the platform is expanding to heat pump installations, electrical panel upgrades, and accessory dwelling units (ADUs).

### Headline Claim

9. "Permits in Seconds!" — Symbium homepage tagline.

10. Bakersfield launched instant permitting with Symbium "for a wide range of energy-saving projects."

## TestFit (testfit.io)

### Company Profile
- **Founders**: Clifton Harness (CEO/Co-founder), Ryan Griege (CTO/Co-founder)
- **Origin**: Harness was working at a development company, "frustrated endlessly drawing apartment units and counting parking stalls." Reached out to college roommate Griege to build software.
- **First version**: "A simple command-line utility to help Clifton solve apartment unit mixes. It wasn't fancy. It didn't even have a user interface."
- **Board includes**: Parkway Venture Capital, Prologis (one of the world's largest industrial REITs)
- **Target users**: Developers, architects, urban planners, civil engineers, general contractors, municipalities

### Product Claims
1. "Real estate feasibility platform" using AI for generative site planning.
2. Automates "monotonous, error-prone steps in the deal process, like counting parking stalls, generating multiple design options, calculating yield on cost."
3. Users can define project goals (FAR, unit counts, parking requirements); software generates matching designs in seconds.
4. Explores thousands of potential configurations to find "highest and best use" of a parcel.
5. Expanded from multi-family to mixed-use, single-family, and industrial developments.
6. Integrates with Revit, AutoCAD, SketchUp.
7. "We believe that everyone deserves quality communities."

## Doxel (doxel.ai)

### Company Profile
- **Founder**: Saurabh Ladha (CEO), founded 2015 "after seeing how construction delays impact lives and livelihoods."
- **Background**: Forbes 30 Under 30; Goldman Sachs Top 100 Builders + Innovators Award (2018); Stanford guest lecturer (2019).
- **VP of Product**: Reid Senescu — structural engineer, PhD from Stanford, previously led product at Rhumbix and Enlighted (acquired by Siemens).
- **Head of Engineering**: Will Truong — background in cancer intelligence, government health programs, military intelligence.

### Product Claims
1. "Computer vision powered observability for construction."
2. Teams capture 360-degree video/imagery (hard-hat-mounted) as they walk the site.
3. AI identifies and counts installed construction elements across trades (structural, MEP, architectural).
4. Platform automatically compares "as-built" data against planned BIM model and project schedule.
5. Generates real-time 4D view showing discrepancies between planned and actual work.
6. Replaces "subjective, manual estimation and reporting with data-backed, visual evidence."
7. Serves data centers, health care, life sciences, manufacturing, semiconductors, oil and gas.

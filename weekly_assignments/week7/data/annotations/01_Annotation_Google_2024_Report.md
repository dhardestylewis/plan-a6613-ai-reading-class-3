# Annotation: Google 2024 Environmental Report (Blog Post)
**Source**: Google. (2024, July 2). Google's 2024 Environmental Report.
**URL**: https://blog.google/outreach-initiatives/sustainability/2024-environmental-report/
**Stakeholder**: Corporate (vendor/self-report)
**Conflict of interest**: Google is reporting on its own emissions — incentive to minimize negative framing

## Key Extractions with Line References

### Emissions Data
- "In 2023, our total GHG emissions increased 13% year-over-year, primarily driven by increased data center energy consumption and supply chain emissions." (Blog position 25)
  - Used in writeup: "a 13% year-over-year increase" ✅

### Mitigation Claims
- "Trillium, our sixth-generation Tensor Processing Unit (TPU), which is over 67% more energy-efficient than TPU v5e." (Blog position 22, fn1)
  - Source for fn1: "This calculation is based on internal data, as of May 2024."
  - NOTE: Internal data = no independent audit. Used in writeup with implicit caveat ✅

- "tested practices that our research shows can, when used together, reduce the energy required to train an AI model by up to 100 times and reduce associated emissions by up to 1,000 times." (Blog position 22, fn2)
  - Source for fn2: "The Carbon Footprint of Machine Learning Training Will Plateau, Then Shrink," Computer, vol. 55, July 2022.
  - NOTE: "when used together" = CONDITIONAL. "up to" = MAXIMUM. Used in writeup with conditionality noted ✅

- "A Google-owned and -operated data center is, on average, approximately 1.8 times as energy efficient as a typical enterprise data center." (Blog position 22, fn3)
  - Source for fn3: "According to Google's own analysis" — self-referential claim ✅

- "the average annual power usage effectiveness for our data centers was 1.10 compared with the industry average of 1.58" (Blog position 22, fn4)
  - Source for fn4: Uptime Institute's 2023 Global Data Center Survey
  - Used in writeup ✅

### AI for Sustainability Claims (NOT used in writeup)
- "Fuel-efficient routing... estimated to have helped enable more than 2.9 million metric tons of GHG emissions reductions" (Blog position 22, fn6)
- "AI has the potential to help mitigate 5–10% of global greenhouse gas emissions by 2030" (Blog position 22, fn5 → BCG, 2021)
  - NOT included in writeup — these are "AI as solution" claims that would dilute the emissions analysis

### Operational Progress
- "10 of our grid regions achieved at least 90% CFE, and... we maintained a global average of 64% CFE." (Blog position 24)
- "We signed contracts to purchase approximately four gigawatts of clean energy generation capacity" (Blog position 24)
  - NOT included in writeup — CFE details would require more space; 64% average is below Google's own 100% matching claim

## What This Source Does NOT Address
- Location-based vs. market-based distinction (Google does not surface this)
- Absolute energy consumption in TWh (only relative efficiency claims)
- How per-unit efficiency relates to total volume growth
- Independent verification of any efficiency claim

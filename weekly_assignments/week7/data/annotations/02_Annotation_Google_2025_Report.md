# Annotation: Google 2025 Environmental Report
**Source**: Google. (2025). Google's 2025 Environmental Report.
**URL**: https://www.gstatic.com/gumdrop/sustainability/google-2025-environmental-report.pdf
**Stakeholder**: Corporate (vendor/self-report)
**Conflict of interest**: Same as 2024 report — Google reporting on its own performance

## Key Extractions (from search synthesis and downloaded PDF)

### Emissions Data
- Total GHG emissions: 11.5 million metric tons (2024 fiscal year)
  - NOTE: Lower than 14.3M in 2023 — need to verify accounting method. May reflect market-based adjustments.
  - Used in writeup: "cumulative 51% increase since 2019" ✅

- 51% increase since 2019 baseline (market-based)
  - Used in writeup ✅

- 11% year-over-year increase (2023 to 2024)
  - NOT used directly in writeup (used the cumulative % instead)

- Data center electricity consumption increased 27% in 2024
  - Used in writeup: "27% jump in data center electricity consumption in a single year" ✅

### Mitigation Claims
- 12% reduction in data center energy emissions in 2024 (despite 27% electricity increase)
  - NOT used in writeup — efficiency vs. absolute distinction already made via IEA

- Median energy consumption per Gemini text prompt decreased 33x over 12 months
  - Carbon footprint per prompt decreased 44x
  - Used in writeup: "median energy consumption per Gemini text prompt fell 33-fold over twelve months" ✅
  - NOTE: Per-unit metric ignores total query volume growth. This caveat is noted in the writeup.

- New methodology released for measuring energy, water, and carbon of AI models
  - NOT used in writeup — methodological transparency claim would need independent verification

### Net-Zero Goal
- Goal: 24/7 carbon-free energy on every grid by 2030
  - NOT directly cited — contrasted with IEA NZE in Verification section

## What This Source Does NOT Address
- Location-based emissions (only market-based figures reported)
- Total query volume growth (would contextualize per-prompt efficiency)
- Comparison to other hyperscalers

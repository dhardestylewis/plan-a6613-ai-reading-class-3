Source: Google Blog — Our 2024 Environmental Report
URL: https://blog.google/outreach-initiatives/sustainability/2024-environmental-report/
Accessed: March 3, 2026
Type: Corporate (vendor/self-report)

KEY DATA EXTRACTED:

Emissions:
- Total GHG emissions increased 13% year-over-year in 2023
- Total GHG emissions: 14.3 million metric tons CO2e (2023)
- 48% increase over 2019 baseline
- Primary drivers: increased data center energy consumption and supply chain emissions
- Google acknowledges AI integration poses challenges to emission reduction goals

Mitigation Technologies:
- Trillium (6th-gen TPU): over 67% more energy-efficient than TPU v5e [fn1: internal data, May 2024]
- Tested practices can reduce energy to train AI model by up to 100x and emissions by up to 1,000x [fn2: "Carbon Footprint of ML Training Will Plateau, Then Shrink," Computer, vol. 55, July 2022]
- Google data centers ~1.8x as energy efficient as typical enterprise data center [fn3: internal analysis, 2023 data]
- Average annual PUE of 1.10 vs industry average of 1.58 [fn4: Uptime Institute 2023 Global Data Center Survey]
- Water risk framework introduced for climate-conscious cooling
- 10 grid regions achieved 90%+ CFE; global average 64% CFE
- Signed contracts for ~4 GW clean energy generation capacity
- First-of-kind enhanced geothermal project (Fervo partnership)

AI for Sustainability Claims:
- AI could help mitigate 5-10% of global GHG by 2030 [fn5: BCG, Jan 2021]
- Fuel-efficient routing: estimated 2.9M metric tons GHG reductions (late 2021 to end 2023)
- Flood prediction: AI model predicts floods up to 7 days in advance in 80+ countries
- Green Light: AI for traffic signal optimization, potential 30% reduction in stops, 10% in emissions

Caveats for audit:
- "100x energy reduction" and "1,000x emissions reduction" are CONDITIONAL: "when used together" and cite a 2022 paper
- 1.8x efficiency comparison is Google's OWN analysis against industry averages
- BCG 5-10% claim is from 2021 and generic (not Google-specific)
- PUE 1.10 is impressive but Uptime Institute notes industry average reflects warmer/smaller emerging market DCs

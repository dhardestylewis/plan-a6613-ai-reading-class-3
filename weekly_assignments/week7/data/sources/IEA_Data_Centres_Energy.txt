Source: IEA — Data Centres and Data Transmission Networks
URL: https://www.iea.org/energy-system/buildings/data-centres-and-data-transmission-networks
Accessed: March 3, 2026
Type: Independent/intergovernmental (policy authority)

KEY DATA EXTRACTED:

Global Context:
- Data centres + data transmission networks: ~1% of energy-related GHG emissions
- 330 Mt CO2e in 2020 (incl. embodied emissions) = 0.9% of energy-related GHG
- Since 2010, emissions have grown modestly despite rapidly growing demand (efficiency gains)
- Global DC electricity consumption in 2022: 240-340 TWh (~1-1.3% of global final electricity demand)
- Excludes crypto mining (~110 TWh in 2022, 0.4% of global electricity demand)

Hyperscaler Trend:
- Combined electricity use of Amazon, Microsoft, Google, and Meta more than doubled 2017-2021
- Rose to ~72 TWh in 2021
- Large data centres growing 20-40% annually in energy use
- Overall DC energy use likely to continue growing moderately near-term; long-term highly uncertain

Context for Google Analysis:
- Hyperscaler growth is outpacing efficiency gains at the aggregate level
- Country-level impacts: Ireland DCs = 18% of total electricity (2022)
- NZE Scenario requires emissions to HALVE by 2030 — sets benchmark against Google's net-zero goal
- Efficiency gains at facility level being overwhelmed by volume growth — a pattern Google exhibits

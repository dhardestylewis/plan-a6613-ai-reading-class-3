# AI Reading Exercise - Week 7

We will again test the power and limitations of modern AI tools for sourcing and synthesizing relevant information. As usual, the objective is to use an LLM to answer a core question related to this week's class session, while critically auditing the tool's output.

For this week's topic of "AI, Energy, and Climate," we will use an LLM to investigate the carbon footprint of a major "hyperscaler" and audit the specific AI-driven technologies they claim will help reduce or offset their emissions. Use an AI tool of your choice (e.g., Gemini, ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) to research the following question:

"According to their latest sustainability report, how much has [Pick one: Google, Microsoft, or Amazon] seen their location-based carbon emissions increase due to AI, and what specific technologies are they using to mitigate this impact?"

## Requirements

Please create a brief (max 1-page) document with bulleted responses to the following:

**Tool & Process**: State which AI model you used and the specific prompt(s) you found most effective.

**Key Findings**: A concise summary of the emissions impact and mitigation approaches identified by the AI.

**Verification (The "Human-in-the-Loop" Check)**:
* Provide three real-world citations (links to corporate-issued sustainability reports, policy reports, academic papers, or news articles) that support these findings.

Crucial: Did the AI identify the company's actual sustainability report? Did it suggest a source that doesn't exist? If the LLM performed any calculations on your behalf, were they correct? Note any "hallucinations" or inaccuracies here.

**Critical Reflection**: In 2-3 sentences, rate the utility of the AI's response. Was it a helpful starting point, or did it require too much "babysitting" to be useful for a planner?

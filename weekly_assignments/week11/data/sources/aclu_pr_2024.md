# Historic Settlement Reached in Defective Facial Recognition Police Wrongful Arrest Case

**Source Type:** Advocacy  
**Organization:** American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) of Michigan  
**Date:** June 28, 2024  

## Press Release

DETROIT – Today, the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) of Michigan, the national ACLU, and the University of Michigan Law School’s Civil Rights Litigation Initiative (CRLI) announced a historic settlement in the wrongful arrest lawsuit of Robert Williams against the City of Detroit. 

In January 2020, Mr. Williams, a Black man, was wrongfully arrested and locked in a crowded, filthy cell overnight after the Detroit Police Department (DPD) relied on flawed facial recognition technology (FRT) that incorrectly identified him as a shoplifting suspect. 

The $300,000 settlement establishes some of the strongest regulations nationwide on police use of FRT. The technology is notorious for racial bias, consistently displaying higher false-positive error rates for people with darker skin tones than for white people. 

Under the new 2024 legal agreement, the Detroit Police Department is formally stripped of the ability to use an FRT "match" as the sole basis for an arrest. The settlement acknowledges the systemic dangers of algorithmic bias and prohibits the DPD from relying on FRT matches to create photographic lineups, a practice that heavily biases eyewitnesses. FRT matches must now be treated merely as uncorroborated "investigative tips."

"This settlement is a massive win for civil rights," said Phil Mayor, Senior Staff Attorney at the ACLU of Michigan. "It forces the Detroit Police to acknowledge that this technology is structurally flawed and cannot be trusted to independently deprive individuals of their liberty."

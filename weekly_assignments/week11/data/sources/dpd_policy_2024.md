# Detroit Police Department Manual: Facial Recognition Technology (Directive 304.1)

**Source Type:** Government / Policy Document  
**Organization:** Detroit Police Department (DPD)  
**Date:** Revised Post-June 2024 Settlement  

## Policy Directive

**1. Purpose**
The purpose of this directive is to establish guidelines for the appropriate use of Facial Recognition Technology (FRT) within the Detroit Police Department. The DPD recognizes the utility of FRT as an investigative tool, but strictly balances this with constitutional protections and the limitations of algorithmic accuracy.

**2. Investigative Lead Generator**
Facial recognition provides a tip or an investigative lead. Under no circumstances does a facial recognition search result inherently establish probable cause for an arrest or a search warrant. 

**3. Prohibited Uses**
Following the 2024 settlement resolution, members of the DPD are strictly prohibited from:
a. Establishing probable cause solely based on a facial recognition lead.
b. Utilizing an FRT match image in a photographic array or lineup to present to an eyewitness.
c. Arresting an individual without substantial independent corroboration linking the suspect to the crime.

**4. Independent Corroboration**
An FRT match must be verified through independent evidence, which may include physical evidence, untainted eyewitness testimony, or digital forensics not derived from the original algorithmic search.

**5. Training and Oversight**
Officers utilizing the FRT database must complete specialized training on algorithmic bias, particularly the elevated error rates associated with low-quality imagery and demographic disparities. All FRT queries are subject to audit by the Board of Police Commissioners to ensure compliance with this directive.

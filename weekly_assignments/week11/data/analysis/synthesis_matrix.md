# Synthesis Matrix: DPD Facial Recognition

## Construct 1: The Function of Facial Recognition
*   **Vendor Perspective:** Biometric signature matching against databases offers rapid identification to "clear investigative backlogs" efficiently. (`[Database Integration]`, `[Mechanism]`) - DataWorks Plus
*   **Advocacy Perspective:** The technology is "structurally flawed" with notorious racial bias and "higher false-positive error rates for people with darker skin tones." (`[Algorithmic Bias]`) - ACLU
*   **Policy Convergence:** The state now formally limits FRT outputs, treating them "merely as uncorroborated 'investigative tips'" rather than absolute identification. (`[Lead Generation]`, `[Policy Limitation]`) - ACLU / DPD Directive

## Construct 2: The "Human-in-the-Loop" Liability Loophole
*   **Vendor Perspective:** Systems rely on humans by delivering "a ranked list of potential matches, allowing human investigators to make the final determination." (`[Human-in-the-Loop]`) - DataWorks Plus
*   **Advocacy Perspective:** Without strict policy, the "human-in-the-loop" fails, leading to false arrests when police rely on the match as the "sole basis for an arrest" or use it "to create photographic lineups." (`[Eyewitness Bias]`) - ACLU
*   **Policy Convergence:** "Human-in-the-loop" must now possess "specialized training on algorithmic bias" and is "strictly prohibited from... Utilizing an FRT match image in a photographic array or lineup to present to an eyewitness" because standard human oversight fails to correct algorithmic bias. (`[Training]`, `[Investigative Protocol]`) - DPD Directive

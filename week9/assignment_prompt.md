AI Reading Exercise - Week 9
We will again test the power and limitations of modern AI tools for sourcing and synthesizing relevant information. As usual, the objective is to use an LLM to answer a core question related to this week's class session, while critically auditing the tool’s output.

For this week's topic of "AI and City Services," we will use an LLM to investigate how a specific city is deploying AI to improve a civic service.  Use an AI tool of your choice (e.g., Gemini, ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) to research the following question:

"How is [pick one city: New York City, Los Angeles, Seattle, or Chicago] currently using AI to improve a specific city service (e.g., permitting, 311 resolution, procurement, or benefits delivery)? What has the city reported about outcomes, and what concerns have residents, advocates, or oversight bodies raised?"

Requirements
Please create a brief (max 1-page) document with bulleted responses to the following:

Tool & Process: State which AI model you used and the specific prompt(s) you found most effective.

Key Findings: A concise summary of the city service, how AI is being applied, and any reported outcomes or impacts the AI identified.

Verification (The "Human-in-the-Loop" Check): * Provide three real-world citations (links to corporate-issued sustainability reports, policy reports, academic papers, or news articles) that support these findings.

Crucial: Did the AI point you to real, retrievable sources? Did it hallucinate a program, policy, or outcome that doesn't exist? Note any inaccuracies here.

Critical Reflection: In 2-3 sentences, rate the utility of the AI’s response. Was it a useful entry point for understanding how cities are adopting AI services, or did it oversimplify, editorialize, or miss important context a planner would need?

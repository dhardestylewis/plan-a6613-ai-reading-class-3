# Source: Partnership for New York City (2024)
**Title:** Return to Office Survey — May 2024
**Author:** Partnership for New York City
**Date:** May 2024
**URL:** https://www.pfnyc.org/

## Key Findings (verified from web search cross-referencing pfnyc.org and nyc.gov)

**Average Daily Attendance:** 56% of Manhattan office workers were in their workplace on an average weekday as of May 2024.

This represents 72% of pre-pandemic attendance levels (pre-pandemic baseline: 78% of workers on an average weekday).

**Weekly Office Presence Breakdown:**
- 5 days/week (fully in-person): 11%
- 4 days/week: 17%
- 3 days/week: 38%
- 2 days/week: 17%
- 1 day/week: 9%
- Fully remote: 7%

**Employer Policies:**
- 60% of employers operated on a hybrid schedule
- 34% had a mix of hybrid, remote, and full-time in-office roles
- 67% of companies had not changed their return-to-office policy in the prior 12 months
- 30% had increased office attendance requirements

**Industry Variation:**
- Real estate: 83% average daily attendance (highest)
- Law: 63%
- Financial services: 60%

## Correction Applied to Memo

The original memo text stated "65 to 75 percent of its pre-pandemic baseline." The verified figure is 72% (56% current vs. ~78% pre-pandemic). The memo's direction of claim is correct but the specific range was slightly overstated. Memo language has been updated to reflect the verified figure.

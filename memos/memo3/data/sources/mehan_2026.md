## Key Excerpts (verified from fetched source)

Abstract: "Artificial Intelligence is increasingly embedded in urban mobility and emergency response systems, where real-time decision-making, infrastructure coordination, and public safety converge."

The paper develops a theoretical framework distinguishing among cognitive, data-driven, and hybrid Urban AI models — examining how each shapes "urban knowledge production, operational performance, and accountability."

Three U.S.-based case studies: (1) AI-enabled emergency vehicle preemption in Fremont, CA; (2) AI-assisted subway infrastructure monitoring in NYC (MTA + Google TrackInspect pilot); (3) AI-driven signal coordination for emergency routing in Seattle.

Core argument: "while Urban AI can significantly improve urban operations, its long-term legitimacy depends on integrating principles of equity, transparency, environmental responsibility, and participatory governance."

Concludes by calling for "integrated Urban AI models that balance technical effectiveness with democratic oversight."

Keywords: urban artificial intelligence, emergency response, infrastructure monitoring, equity, sustainability.

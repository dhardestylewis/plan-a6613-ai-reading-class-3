## Key Excerpts and Findings (verified from fetched source)

The 2023 Landscape report is AI Now Institute's annual diagnostic of how concentrated tech power shapes AI deployment across public and private sectors. It is organized around structural policy reforms, not just technical audits.

The report's key sections: Algorithmic Accountability (moving beyond audits), Algorithmic Management (workplace surveillance), Biometric Surveillance, Antitrust/Competition, and Toxic Data Competition.

Core argument: the risk of AI is not primarily technical error — it is structural concentration of power. Platforms that generate AI-optimized outputs do not merely reflect existing data; they generate self-reinforcing feedback loops that calcify the distributions they were trained on.

The Algorithmic Accountability section argues that audits are insufficient. What's needed are binding pre-deployment requirements and public oversight mechanisms — the same argument applied to transit AI and municipal AI services throughout the course.

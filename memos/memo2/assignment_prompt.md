The Challenge
Cities are increasingly turning to AI to modernize the delivery of government services, from permitting and procurement to 311 resolution and benefits administration. But deploying AI in civic contexts raises hard questions about effectiveness, equity, sustainability, and accountability.

For this memo, you will move beyond theory and engage directly with an AI-powered government service as both an analyst and a user. Your first task is to select one AI-powered government service that is currently operational, in pilot, or available as a public demo or simulation. Examples include:

Permitting & Licensing: e.g., a generative AI assistant for zoning or building permit applications.
Resident Services: e.g., an AI-powered 311 chatbot or benefits eligibility tool.
Inspections & Code Enforcement: e.g., a predictive model for prioritizing building or restaurant inspections.
Memo Structure
I. Service Selection & How It Works
Briefly describe the AI-powered service you selected and your firsthand experience using it:

What city function does it serve, and what problem is it designed to solve?
What AI approach does it use (e.g., large language model, predictive model, computer vision), and what data does it rely on?
What was your experience as a user? What worked well, and where did the product fall short?

II. Service Delivery: Benefits and Risks
Evaluate whether this use of AI improves upon the status quo, guided by the following questions:

Effectiveness: Does the AI deliver faster, more accurate, or more consistent outcomes than a traditional approach? What evidence (quantitative and/or qualitative) supports or undermines this claim?
Equity: Who benefits from this service, and who may be left behind? Consider differential access, algorithmic bias, and whether the tool disadvantages particular communities or languages.
City Workers: How does the AI change the role of the public employees who previously delivered or supported this service? Does it augment their work, displace it, or create new responsibilities?
III. Viability & Governance

Drawing on class discussions and readings to date, assess the longer-term viability of this approach at scale:

Financial Sustainability: What does it cost to build, deploy, and maintain this system? How might costs and savings evolve if the city were to expand the program citywide?
Accountability: Who can be held responsible when the AI makes a mistake? What oversight mechanisms exist, and are they sufficient?
Looking Ahead: If this service were adopted at scale, how would the day-to-day experience of residents and city workers change? What policy guardrails or governance reforms would you recommend to maximize benefits and mitigate risks?

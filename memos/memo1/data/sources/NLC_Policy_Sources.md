## National League of Cities (2024)
- Published "Data Centers and Local Environmental Considerations" report
- Hosted webinar on "Datacenter Impacts on Municipal Energy, Water, and Air Systems"
- Key findings:
  - Data centers offer economic benefits (tax revenue, temporary construction jobs)
  - Concerns: water and energy consumption, GHG emissions, grid strain, noise and light pollution
  - Local governments increasingly considering zoning ordinances for data center regulation
  - Prioritizing commercial and industrial zones for data center siting
  - Coordinated planning needed for power grid expansions and water distribution
  - Public education needed about energy/water demands of AI and cloud services

## AI Policy Bulletin (2024)
- Local pushback has delayed or blocked billions in data center projects in the U.S.
- Many communities concerned about electricity/water consumption, noise pollution, impact on utility rates
- Traditional real estate legal frameworks struggling to keep pace with data center development
- Growing need for policy alignment across federal, state, and local governments

## Kiowa County Press (2024)
- Denver considered moratorium on new data center construction in 2024
- Purpose: allow policymakers time to review and update regulations
- Ensure alignment with community values and environmental goals

# Pew Research Center: What we know about energy use at U.S. data centers amid the AI boom
**Source URL**: https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/10/24/what-we-know-about-energy-use-at-us-data-centers-amid-the-ai-boom/
**Source type**: Nonpartisan research organization (Pew Research Center)
**Author**: Rebecca Leppert
**Published**: October 24, 2025
**Downloaded**: March 3, 2026 via read_url_content

## Key Data Points Verified

### Energy Consumption
- U.S. data centers consumed 183 TWh of electricity in 2024 (citing IEA)
- Over 4% of total U.S. electricity consumption
- Equivalent to Pakistan's annual electricity demand
- Projected 133% growth to 426 TWh by 2030
- Typical AI-focused hyperscaler consumes as much electricity as 100,000 households (citing IEA)
- Largest under construction: 20x that amount

### Geographic Concentration
- Over 4,000 data centers in U.S. (Data Center Map)
- Top 3 states: Virginia (643), Texas (395), California (319)
- Half of data centers being built are in pre-existing large clusters (citing IEA)
- In 2023, data centers consumed ~26% of Virginia's total electricity supply (citing EPRI)

### Energy Sources
- Natural gas: over 40% of U.S. data center electricity in 2024 (citing IEA)
- Renewables (wind/solar): ~24%
- Nuclear: ~20%
- Coal: ~15%

### Electricity Bills
- PJM electricity market: data centers caused $9.3 billion capacity market price increase (citing IEEFA)
- Average residential bill expected to rise $18/month in western Maryland, $16/month in Ohio
- Carnegie Mellon study: 8% average U.S. bill increase by 2030; exceeding 25% in central/northern Virginia
- U.S. household average billed $142/month in 2024 (up 25% from $114 in 2014) (citing EIA)

### Water Usage
- 17 billion gallons direct water consumed by U.S. data centers in 2023 (citing Berkeley Lab 2024)
- Hyperscale data centers alone: 16-33 billion gallons/year by 2028

### Methodology Note
- Analysis primarily based on IEA "Energy and AI" report (April 2025)
- Data center counts from Data Center Map (as of Oct 20, 2025)
- Household cost data from EIA
- Survey data from Aug 12-18, 2024 survey of 5,410 U.S. adults

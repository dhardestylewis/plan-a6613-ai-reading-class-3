# Annotation: Community Impact and Regulatory Response
**Sources**: Northern Virginia Magazine, ARLnow, Cardinal News, Inside Climate News, American Action Forum, 13NewsNow, NLC, AI Policy Bulletin, Kiowa County Press

## Stakeholder Analysis

### Local Government Perspective
- Loudoun County: $600M tax revenue in FY2023 — enormous fiscal incentive to host data centers
- Denver moratorium consideration: illustrates how cities can use zoning authority as a brake
- NLC: municipalities balancing economic benefits against environmental externalities
- Jurisdictional gap: cities have zoning authority but not rate-setting or transmission siting power

### Business/Operator Perspective
- Modular facility designs as response to rapid AI technology cycles
- Renewable energy pledges vs. actual grid mix (40% gas, 24% renewable in U.S.)
- GS-5 rate class (14-year contracts) forces operators to internalize long-term grid costs
- AI Policy Bulletin: billions in projects delayed or blocked by local pushback

### Resident Perspective
- Three distinct roles identified: ratepayers, neighbors, environmental stakeholders
- 100%+ bill spikes in Northern Virginia (ARLnow)
- $16/month Dominion rate increase (Inside Climate News)
- 430+ hours potential outages by 2030 vs. 2.4 hours currently
- Organized opposition: Coalition to Protect Prince William County, Virginia Data Center Reform Coalition
- Water consumption concerns during drought conditions

### Regulatory/Legislative Perspective
- Virginia SCC as key decision-maker (not city government)
- GS-5 rate class: structural reform to shift costs to large consumers
- SB 253: legislative attempt to further shift infrastructure costs
- Federal role: FERC, DOE on interstate transmission; White House executive order on data center permitting

## Key Tension Identified
The level of government closest to affected residents (the city) has the least power over the infrastructure's most consequential impacts (electricity prices, transmission siting, generation mix). This jurisdictional mismatch is the central governance challenge.

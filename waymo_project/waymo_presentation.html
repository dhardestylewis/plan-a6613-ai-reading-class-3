Part 1: Product — Jiayi

What is Waymo?

Formerly the Google Self-Driving Car Project, Waymo operates a fully autonomous (Level 4) ride-hailing service.

The Mission

To make it safe and easy for people and things to get where they're going, eliminating human driving errors (responsible for 94% of crashes).

Technology Stack

  • LiDAR: 3D spatial mapping
  • Radar: Distance/speed in all weather
  • Cameras: Visual context (signs, lights)
  • Heavy reliance on high-fidelity, pre-mapped environments.

Current Deployments & Market

Commercial Zones

Market Position

Waymo is currently the clear market leader in the US robotaxi sector, scaling commercial operations while competitors face regulatory setbacks.

Status: Transitioning from R&D to scaled public utility.

Part 1: Existing Context — Jiayi

User Experience & Critiques

While safety records are strong, the human-machine interaction introduces new friction points.

The "Empty Driver's Seat" Dilemma

Users report feeling awkward or anxious without a human presence.

  • Proposal: Implement a chatbot agent/avatar in the driver's seat screen to humanize the experience.
  • Edge Case Challenge: Passenger capacity limits (e.g., 5 people in a 4-person ride trying to use the driver's seat). Requires better warning systems and UI guidance during emergencies.

Part 2: Impact Analysis — Daniel

Physical Planning

Equity & Infrastructure

(UC Berkeley, 2025): Waymo service zones often mirror historical disparities. Better roads get AVs first, risking a "two-tiered" mobility system.

Energy Paradox

(MIT, 2025): "Data centers on wheels." Electric fleet avoids tailpipe emissions, but the compute power for autonomy requires massive grid energy.

Safety & Transit

92%

Fewer pedestrian injuries compared to human drivers (Waymo, 2026).

Potential to fill "transit mobility gaps" for seniors (Valley Metro pilot).

Part 2: Impact Analysis — Daniel

Workforce Development

Job Creation

+114k

Projected new jobs in AV production, maintenance, and remote operations over 15 years (Chamber of Progress, 2024).

Labor Displacement

  • 85% of surveyed adults fear significant job losses for ride-hailing/delivery drivers (UCSD, 2026).
  • Direct exposure to AV tech correlates with declines in commercial driver licensing (AEA).
Part 2: Impact Analysis — Daniel

Data & Privacy

Surveillance Concerns

Vehicles capture vast amounts of "urban spatial data," raising concerns about increased street-level surveillance of neighborhoods (UC).

Regulatory Confidentiality

Waymo requests keeping operational data confidential from regulators, citing trade secrets (SF Examiner).

Data Transparency

Maintains an Open Dataset to share sensor data with researchers to improve safety perception models.

Part 3: Recommendations — June

Policy & Design Recommendations

Focusing on the Physical Planning domain.

The Core Challenge

How do we integrate AVs into the urban fabric to maximize safety and accessibility, without exacerbating spatial inequities, stressing the power grid, or cannibalizing existing public transit networks?

Part 3: Recommendations — June

1. Equitable Service Zones

Mandate a "Mobility Equity Framework" for autonomous operators.

  • Link expansion in high-income/high-demand areas with proportional service deployment in underserved neighborhoods.
  • Condition operating permits on serving transit-challenged areas.

Infrastructure Support

Municipalities should provide targeted infrastructure upgrades (e.g., improved road markings, signage) in lower-income areas to facilitate safe and reliable AV operation where the physical environment is currently lacking.

Part 3: Recommendations — June

2. AV-Transit Hubs

Design for First/Last Mile connectivity, not direct transit competition.

Physical Design

Redesign infrastructure around major transit stations to include dedicated, geofenced AV pick-up/drop-off zones, repurposing curbside parking.

Pricing Policy

Implement geofencing regulations that mandate lower pricing for AV trips that originate or terminate at high-capacity transit stations.

Part 3: Recommendations — June

3. Grid Integration & V2I

Energy Management

Address the "data center on wheels" demand by zoning AV depots in industrial areas with robust electrical capacity.

Mandate off-peak charging schedules or local renewable energy generation at fleet hubs.

Vehicle-to-Infrastructure (V2I)

Allocate infrastructure funds to upgrade traffic signals and intersections to communicate directly with the AV fleet.

Standardize data sharing to continuously monitor intersection safety.

Conclusion

The Future of Urban Mobility

If regulated correctly, Waymo isn't just a technology product; it becomes a piece of public-private transit infrastructure that makes cities safer, more connected, and more accessible.

# Waymo Presentation: Speaker Notes & Outline

**Total Time:** ~20 minutes
**Team:** Jiayi, Daniel, June

## Part 1: Product Overview (Jiayi)
**Time:** 3-4 minutes

### Slide 1: Introduction & Background
- **Talking Points:**
  - Introduce Waymo (formerly the Google Self-Driving Car Project).
  - **Goal/Mission:** To make it safe and easy for people and things to get where they're going.
  - Why build this? Address human error in driving (which causes 94% of crashes), improve accessibility for those who cannot drive, and reclaim time spent commuting.

### Slide 2: Technology & Functionality
- **Talking Points:**
  - **The "Waymo Driver":** Explain the hardware/software stack. It doesn't rely on a single sensor.
  - Uses LiDAR (3D mapping), radar (speed/distance in all weather), and high-res cameras (visual context like stoplights and signs).
  - **Differentiation:** Unlike Tesla's vision-only approach or standard ride-hailing (Uber/Lyft), Waymo provides a Level 4 fully autonomous service (no human in the driver's seat) using heavy, pre-mapped infrastructure and sensor redundancy.

### Slide 3: Recent Milestones
- **Talking Points:**
  - Rapid expansion in the last 2 years.
  - Moving from testing to fully commercialized 24/7 operations in multiple cities without safety drivers.
  - Discuss the latest 6th-generation hardware which reduces costs while improving performance in harsh weather.

## Part 2: Existing Context & Status (Jiayi)
**Time:** 2-3 minutes

### Slide 4: Current Deployment & Market
- **Talking Points:**
  - **Deployments:** Operating commercially in Phoenix (Waymo One), San Francisco, Los Angeles, and Austin.
  - **Market Trend:** The robotaxi market is expanding rapidly, transitioning from R&D to scaling commercial operations. Waymo is currently the clear market leader in the US after Cruise's setbacks.

### Slide 5: User Experience & Critiques
- **Talking Points:**
  - Overall, users report feeling very safe, though sometimes the driving style is overly cautious.
  - **Critique/Thought:** The empty driver's seat can feel awkward or unsettling.
  - **Idea:** What if there was a chatbot agent or avatar in the driver's seat screen to humanize the experience? 
  - **Edge Cases:** What happens if 5 people try to cram into a 4-passenger ride and someone sits in the driver's seat? Waymo has internal cameras and weight sensors to detect this and will halt the ride, but UI/UX communication around these rules could be clearer in emergencies.

## Part 3: Analysis of Impacts (Daniel)
**Time:** 6-8 minutes

### Slide 6: Impact Overview
- **Talking Points:**
  - Transitioning from the product itself to its broader societal footprint. We're looking at three main domains: Physical Planning, Workforce Development, and Data & Privacy.

### Slide 7: Physical Planning & Infrastructure
- **Talking Points:**
  - **Equity:** (UC Berkeley, 2025) Waymo's deployment maps often reflect existing infrastructure disparities. Better roads get AVs first, risking a "two-tiered" mobility system.
  - **Safety:** (Safety Impact Report, 2026) The data is strong—92% fewer pedestrian injuries, 96% fewer intersection crashes. A huge win for Vision Zero.
  - **Energy:** (MIT, 2025) The "Data Centers on Wheels" paradox. The compute power needed for autonomy is massive. While EVs are clean locally, scaling this requires massive grid capacity.
  - **Transit:** Pilot with Valley Metro shows promise in filling first/last-mile gaps, especially for seniors and paratransit riders.

### Slide 8: Workforce Development
- **Talking Points:**
  - **Job Creation:** (Chamber of Progress, 2024) Projection of 114,000+ new jobs in fleet maintenance, remote assistance, and vehicle production over 15 years.
  - **Displacement & Anxiety:** (UC San Diego, 2026) 85% of adults fear massive job losses for ride-hail/delivery drivers. 
  - **Labor Market Shift:** (AEA Working Paper) Direct exposure to AV tech correlates with a decline in commercial driver licensing. The transition period will be highly disruptive for gig workers.

### Slide 9: Data and Privacy
- **Talking Points:**
  - **Surveillance:** Waymo vehicles are effectively rolling sensor arrays capturing "urban spatial data." This raises concerns about neighborhood surveillance.
  - **Regulation vs. Secrecy:** (SF Examiner) Waymo frequently requests data confidentiality from regulators citing trade secrets, which conflicts with public interest in safety and traffic data.
  - **Transparency:** They do maintain an Open Dataset for researchers, but the line between proprietary operational data and public good data is still being fought over.

### Slide 10: Summary of Impacts
- **Talking Points:**
  - Briefly synthesize: Waymo offers immense safety benefits but requires careful management of equity, labor transitions, and privacy rights. This leads us to our policy recommendations.

## Part 4: Policy & Design Recommendations (June)
**Time:** 6-8 minutes

### Slide 11: Framing the Core Issue
- **Talking Points:**
  - **Focus:** We are focusing our recommendations on the **Physical Planning** domain.
  - **The Core Issue:** How do we integrate AVs into the city without exacerbating spatial inequities or cannibalizing public transit?

### Slide 12: Recommendation 1 - Equitable Service Zones
- **Talking Points:**
  - Municipalities must mandate a "Mobility Equity Framework." 
  - If Waymo wants to operate in high-revenue areas, they must commit to proportional service in underserved or transit-desert neighborhoods. Subsidies might be needed to improve physical infrastructure in these areas to support AVs.

### Slide 13: Recommendation 2 - AV-Transit Hubs
- **Talking Points:**
  - Design intervention: We must design for First/Last Mile, not direct competition.
  - Create dedicated, geofenced AV staging areas at major transit stations. 
  - Use pricing mechanisms: AV rides connecting to a train station should be cheaper than door-to-door trips that run parallel to a train line.

### Slide 14: Recommendation 3 - Infrastructure & Grid Integration
- **Talking Points:**
  - Address the "data center on wheels" energy demand.
  - Planners need to zone large AV charging depots in areas with robust industrial power grids, away from fragile residential grids. Require off-peak charging.
  - Invest in V2I (Vehicle-to-Infrastructure): Smart traffic lights that talk directly to the Waymo fleet to optimize traffic flow and safety.

### Slide 15: Conclusion & Future Vision
- **Talking Points:**
  - **Stakeholders:** This requires collaboration between Waymo, local DOTs, transit agencies, and the public.
  - **Vision:** If regulated correctly, Waymo isn't just a tech product; it becomes a piece of public-private transit infrastructure that makes cities safer and more connected. 
  - Open the floor for questions.
